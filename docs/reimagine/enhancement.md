## What is Image Enhancement?

Image enhancement uses AI to analyze and improve photo quality automatically. It corrects common issues like poor lighting, dull colors, and lack of sharpness to create professional-looking images.

### Manual Adjustment Mode

For fine-tuned control over specific parameters:

#### Brightness (-100 to +100)

**What it does**: Adjusts overall image lightness

**When to adjust:**

- **Increase (+)**: Dark photos, underexposed images, shadow-heavy rooms
- **Decrease (-)**: Overexposed photos, too-bright windows, washed-out images

**Recommended ranges:**

- **Subtle**: ¬±10 to ¬±20
- **Moderate**: ¬±20 to ¬±40
- **Strong**: ¬±40 to ¬±60
- **Extreme**: ¬±60 to ¬±100 (use sparingly)

**Tips:**

- Start with small adjustments (+10 or -10)
- Watch for loss of detail in highlights/shadows
- Combine with contrast for best results

---

#### Contrast (-100 to +100)

**What it does**: Adjusts difference between light and dark areas

**When to adjust:**

- **Increase (+)**: Flat, dull images, foggy conditions, low-impact photos
- **Decrease (-)**: Harsh shadows, overly dramatic images, blown highlights

**Recommended ranges:**

- **Subtle**: ¬±5 to ¬±15
- **Moderate**: ¬±15 to ¬±30
- **Strong**: ¬±30 to ¬±50
- **Extreme**: ¬±50 to ¬±100 (rarely needed)

**Tips:**

- Increasing contrast adds "pop" to images
- Too much contrast loses shadow/highlight detail
- Pair with brightness for balanced results

---

#### Saturation (-100 to +100)

**What it does**: Adjusts color intensity and vibrancy

**When to adjust:**

- **Increase (+)**: Dull colors, overcast day photos, lifeless images
- **Decrease (-)**: Oversaturated images, unnatural colors, garish tones

**Recommended ranges:**

- **Subtle**: ¬±5 to ¬±15
- **Moderate**: ¬±15 to ¬±25
- **Strong**: ¬±25 to ¬±40
- **Extreme**: ¬±40 to ¬±100 (avoid for realism)

**Tips:**

- Real estate photos should look natural
- Avoid over-saturation (looks fake)
- Slight increase (+10 to +15) works for most photos
- Decrease for more sophisticated, muted look

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue: Image looks over-processed or fake

**Cause**: Too much adjustment applied
**Solution**:

- Reduce all parameters by 50%
- Use auto-adjust instead
- Aim for natural, realistic look
- Less is more in real estate

### Issue: Colors look unnatural

**Cause**: Excessive saturation increase
**Solution**:

- Reduce saturation to +10 to +15
- Check original photo white balance
- Use auto-adjust for balanced colors

### Issue: Lost detail in bright areas

**Cause**: Too much brightness or contrast
**Solution**:

- Reduce brightness adjustment
- Lower contrast slightly
- Original may be overexposed (retake if possible)

### Issue: Image still looks dark

**Cause**: Original severely underexposed
**Solution**:

- Increase brightness more (+50 to +70)
- Increase contrast (+20 to +30)
- Consider retaking with better lighting

### Issue: Enhancement didn't help much

**Cause**: Original photo quality too poor
**Solution**:

- Retake photo with better lighting
- Clean camera lens
- Use camera's HDR mode if available
- Consider professional photography
