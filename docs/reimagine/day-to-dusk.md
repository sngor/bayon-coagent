## Before You Start

### Image Requirements

- **Exterior shot** with visible sky
- **Daytime photo** (morning or afternoon preferred)
- **Interior lights ON** for best effect
- **Clear weather** (some clouds okay)
- **Good exposure** (not too dark or bright)

### Best Photo Practices

1. **Shoot in late afternoon** (3-5 PM) for best starting light
2. **Turn on ALL interior lights** before shooting
3. **Turn on exterior lights** (porch, landscape, pool)
4. **Include sky** in the frame (at least 20% of image)
5. **Avoid harsh shadows** from midday sun
6. **Use tripod** for sharp images

## Using Day to Dusk

### Step 1: Choose Intensity Level

Select the transformation intensity that matches your property style:

#### Subtle

- **Light golden hour glow**
- Sky transitions to soft orange/pink
- Interior lights gently visible
- Natural, understated look

**Best for:**

- Daytime listings that need slight enhancement
- Properties in bright, sunny climates
- Conservative markets
- Maintaining realistic appearance

---

#### Moderate (Recommended)

- **Classic golden hour lighting**
- Rich orange and purple sky gradients
- Interior lights clearly visible and warm
- Balanced, professional look

**Best for:**

- Most residential properties
- Standard real estate listings
- Balanced emotional impact
- Professional marketing materials

---

#### Dramatic

- **Deep twilight atmosphere**
- Dark blue sky with vibrant sunset colors
- Interior lights prominently glowing
- High-impact, luxurious feel

**Best for:**

- Luxury properties
- High-end listings
- Properties with extensive lighting
- Maximum emotional impact
- Social media marketing

### Step 2: Review Results

After processing, check for:

1. **Sky gradient** - Smooth transition from light to dark
2. **Interior light glow** - Warm, inviting window illumination
3. **Exterior lighting** - Landscape and porch lights visible
4. **Color balance** - Natural-looking warm tones
5. **Shadow detail** - Maintained visibility in darker areas

### Step 3: Adjust if Needed

If results need refinement:

- **Too dark?** Try "Moderate" instead of "Dramatic"
- **Too bright?** Try "Dramatic" instead of "Subtle"
- **Unnatural colors?** Ensure original photo has good white balance
- **Lights not visible?** Verify lights were on in original photo

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue: Sky looks unnatural or oversaturated

**Cause**: Original photo had poor white balance or exposure
**Solution**:

- Enhance original photo first
- Try lower intensity setting
- Ensure original has neutral white balance

### Issue: Interior lights not visible

**Cause**: Lights weren't on in original photo
**Solution**:

- Retake photo with all interior lights on
- Ensure curtains/blinds are open
- Shoot during daylight hours (not at night)

### Issue: Image too dark overall

**Cause**: Dramatic intensity on already dark photo
**Solution**:

- Use Moderate or Subtle intensity
- Enhance original photo brightness first
- Shoot earlier in the day

### Issue: Colors look muddy or brown

**Cause**: Original photo had poor color quality
**Solution**:

- Apply enhancement first
- Ensure original has good color saturation
- Check camera white balance settings

### Issue: Harsh shadows remain

**Cause**: Midday photo with strong shadows
**Solution**:

- Shoot in late afternoon instead
- Use enhancement to soften shadows first
- Avoid shooting between 11 AM - 2 PM
