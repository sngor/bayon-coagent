## Photography Best Practices

### Equipment

**Minimum Requirements:**

- Smartphone with 12MP+ camera
- Steady hands or basic tripod
- Good lighting conditions

**Recommended:**

- DSLR or mirrorless camera
- Wide-angle lens (16-35mm)
- Sturdy tripod
- External flash or lighting

**Professional:**

- Full-frame camera
- Professional wide-angle lens
- Professional tripod with level
- Multiple light sources
- HDR bracketing capability

---

### Camera Settings

**For Smartphones:**

- Use highest quality setting
- Enable HDR mode
- Turn off digital zoom
- Use grid lines for composition
- Clean lens before shooting

**For DSLR/Mirrorless:**

- **ISO**: 100-400 (lowest possible)
- **Aperture**: f/8-f/11 (sharp throughout)
- **Shutter Speed**: 1/60s or faster (use tripod if slower)
- **White Balance**: Auto or Daylight
- **Format**: RAW for maximum flexibility

---

### Lighting

**Natural Light (Best):**

- Shoot during "golden hours" (morning/late afternoon)
- Open all curtains and blinds
- Turn on all interior lights
- Avoid harsh midday sun
- Overcast days provide even lighting

**Interior Lighting:**

- Turn on ALL lights in the room
- Use consistent color temperature bulbs
- Avoid mixing warm and cool lights
- Add supplemental lighting if needed
- Balance with natural light

**Exterior Lighting:**

- Best: Late afternoon (3-5 PM)
- Good: Morning (8-10 AM)
- Avoid: Midday (11 AM-2 PM)
- Turn on exterior lights for dusk shots
- Ensure even lighting across facade

---

### Composition

**Rule of Thirds:**

- Place key features at intersection points
- Don't center everything
- Create visual interest
- Guide viewer's eye

**Angles and Perspective:**

- Shoot from 5 feet height (eye level)
- Keep camera level (use grid/level)
- Avoid extreme angles
- Shoot corners at 45 degrees
- Include context and scale

**Framing:**

- Include full room in frame
- Show doorways and transitions
- Capture architectural details
- Avoid cutting off features
- Leave some negative space

---

### Room-Specific Tips

**Living Rooms:**

- Shoot from doorway
- Include windows for light
- Show full seating area
- Capture fireplace if present
- Include ceiling height

**Kitchens:**

- Clear countertops
- Open cabinet doors slightly
- Show appliances
- Include island if present
- Capture full work triangle

**Bedrooms:**

- Make bed perfectly
- Remove personal items
- Show closet space
- Include windows
- Capture full room

**Bathrooms:**

- Close toilet lid
- Remove personal items
- Show shower/tub
- Include vanity
- Ensure clean surfaces

**Exteriors:**

- Shoot from street view
- Include landscaping
- Show full facade
- Capture curb appeal
- Include driveway/parking

## Editing Workflow

### Recommended Order

1. **Upload and Review**

   - Upload high-quality original
   - Review AI suggestions
   - Plan editing sequence

2. **Enhancement First**

   - Always enhance before other edits
   - Correct exposure and color
   - Improve overall quality
   - Creates better base for other edits

3. **Item Removal (if needed)**

   - Remove clutter and distractions
   - Clean up unwanted objects
   - Prepare space for staging

4. **Virtual Staging or Renovation**

   - Stage empty rooms
   - Visualize renovations
   - Show potential

5. **Day-to-Dusk (exteriors only)**

   - Apply to enhanced exteriors
   - Create emotional impact
   - Final touch for curb appeal

6. **Final Review**
   - Compare all versions
   - Ensure consistency
   - Check quality standards

---

### Edit Chaining Strategy

**Example Chain 1: Empty Property**

```
Original → Enhancement → Virtual Staging → Accept
```

**Example Chain 2: Exterior Shot**

```
Original → Enhancement → Day-to-Dusk → Accept
```

**Example Chain 3: Cluttered Interior**

```
Original → Enhancement → Item Removal → Virtual Staging → Accept
```

**Example Chain 4: Fixer-Upper**

```
Original → Enhancement → Virtual Renovation → Accept
```

---

### Batch Processing

**For Multiple Similar Photos:**

1. Test edits on one photo first
2. Note successful parameters
3. Apply same edits to similar photos
4. Maintain consistency across set
5. Review all results together

**Consistency Tips:**

- Use same enhancement settings for similar rooms
- Match staging styles across property
- Apply same day-to-dusk intensity to all exteriors
- Keep editing style consistent throughout listing

## Quality Standards

### Professional Image Criteria

✅ **Exposure:**

- Bright but not blown out
- Details visible in shadows
- No harsh highlights
- Even lighting throughout

✅ **Color:**

- Natural and accurate
- Vibrant but not oversaturated
- Consistent white balance
- Appropriate for property style

✅ **Sharpness:**

- Clear and crisp
- No motion blur
- Details well-defined
- Not over-sharpened

✅ **Composition:**

- Well-framed
- Straight lines level
- Appropriate perspective
- Visually balanced

✅ **Realism:**

- Looks natural and believable
- Edits are subtle and professional
- No obvious AI artifacts
- Maintains property integrity

---

### Quality Checklist

Before accepting any edit:

- [ ] Image looks natural and realistic
- [ ] Lighting is appropriate and even
- [ ] Colors are accurate and appealing
- [ ] Details are sharp and clear
- [ ] Composition is professional
- [ ] No obvious editing artifacts
- [ ] Consistent with other listing photos
- [ ] Meets MLS/listing requirements
- [ ] Represents property accurately
- [ ] Would attract target buyers

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Photography Mistakes

❌ **Poor Lighting**

- Shooting in dark conditions
- Not turning on lights
- Harsh shadows from midday sun
- Mixed color temperature lighting

✅ **Solution**: Shoot during golden hours, turn on all lights, use supplemental lighting

---

❌ **Bad Angles**

- Extreme wide-angle distortion
- Shooting from too high or low
- Tilted camera
- Cutting off important features

✅ **Solution**: Shoot from 5 feet height, keep camera level, use standard focal length

---

❌ **Cluttered Spaces**

- Personal items visible
- Messy rooms
- Distracting objects
- Unfinished areas

✅ **Solution**: Clean and stage before shooting, remove personal items, use item removal tool

---

### Editing Mistakes

❌ **Over-Processing**

- Too much brightness/contrast
- Oversaturated colors
- Excessive sharpening
- Unnatural appearance

✅ **Solution**: Use subtle adjustments, aim for natural look, less is more

---

❌ **Inconsistent Editing**

- Different styles across photos
- Varying color tones
- Mixed enhancement levels
- Mismatched staging styles

✅ **Solution**: Batch process similar photos, maintain consistent style, review set together

---

❌ **Unrealistic Edits**

- Impossible staging
- Extreme renovations
- Fake-looking enhancements
- Misleading representations

✅ **Solution**: Keep edits realistic, represent property accurately, maintain trust

---

❌ **Wrong Edit Type**

- Staging furnished rooms
- Day-to-dusk on interiors
- Enhancement on already edited photos
- Renovation for minor updates

✅ **Solution**: Choose appropriate edit type, follow recommended workflow, read guides

---

❌ **Skipping Preview**

- Accepting without review
- Not comparing before/after
- Missing obvious issues
- Rushing the process

✅ **Solution**: Always preview carefully, use comparison slider, check all details
